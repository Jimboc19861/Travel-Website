Welcome To Bruges
© Columbus Travel Media Ltd 2020

A UNESCO World Heritage City, Bruges is a place that will capture your heart and mind.
Sitting as the capital in the Northwest of Belgium and opening out into the North Sea, Bruges is a city full of culture and history with plenty of things to see and do to keep you entertained.

Dating back to the Bronze age and keeping most of its architecture in tact, Bruges is one of the best preserved medieval towns in Europe.

With its picturesque canals, old cobbled lanes and soaring towers and churches, it is easy to think you have walked into a fairytale and its brightly coloured townhouses and huge central market square bring the city to life.

Take a stroll through beautiful Minnewater Park and over the iconic lovers bridge or explore one of the many historic churches and towers before indulging in some local cuisine and world famous Belgium beer.

Riverboats and classic horse and carts can take you around the city and canals giving you a local guide of Bruges that touristic areas wont.

Whether a weekend break or a longer getaway, Bruges has something for everyone.

"I love Bruges. It's so beautiful and colourful and looks good no matter what time of year you go. There are lots of places to go and things to see meaning you are never bored and if you get a chance to do the riverboat tour then you wont regret it" - Maira Suzin

The four seasons all offer something different in Bruges. From the flowers blooming and religious holiday ceremonies in spring, the exhibitions and festivals in summer, the beautiful colours and stalls in autumn and the famous Christmas markets and ice rinks in winter mean whichever time you choose to visit, there will always be something going on!
